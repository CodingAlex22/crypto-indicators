MACD

The Moving Average Convergence-Divergence (MACD) indicator is a tool that traders use to understand the momentum of a trend. It works by comparing two moving averages of an asset's price over time. You can choose any moving average that you prefer for the MACD, but typically traders use exponential moving averages of 26 and 12 days. The MACD is often used on charts for trading cryptocurrencies, especially on a 4-hour or 1-day time frame. The MACD shows the relationship between these two moving averages on a chart. When the shorter moving average (the 12-day average) crosses above the longer moving average (the 26-day average), it can be a bullish signal indicating that the trend is going up. Conversely, when the shorter moving average crosses below the longer moving average, it can be a bearish signal indicating that the trend is going down.

How to use the MACD indicator

When the MACD line moves above the base line, it's considered a bullish signal, indicating an uptrend. Conversely, when the MACD line dips below the zero line, it's considered a bearish signal, indicating a downtrend. Traders also pay attention to when the MACD line crosses above or below the signal line. When the MACD line crosses above the signal line, it can be a sign to buy, and when it crosses below the signal line, it can be a sign to sell. In addition, traders watch for what's called "divergences." A bullish MACD divergence occurs when the price of an asset is trending downwards but the MACD line is indicating higher bottoms. This can be a strong bullish signal that the price may start to increase. Conversely, a bearish MACD divergence occurs when the price is rising but the MACD line is indicating lower tops, which can be a bearish signal that the price may start to decline. Traders also pay attention to when the MACD line moves a significant distance from the signal line. If the MACD line moves a relatively big distance from the signal line, it's considered an overbought MACD signal, and traders may expect the bullish trend to slow down or reverse. On the other hand, if the MACD line secures a relatively significant distance from the signal line, it's considered an oversold MACD signal, and traders may expect the bearish trend to slow down or reverse.
